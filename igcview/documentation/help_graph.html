IGCview Barograph Help

These notes explain how to use the barograph window, which will graphically display your altitude, climb rates and cruise speeds against either time or distance.

The buttons which currently appear down the left side affect the vertical axis and are:

Help. That button shows this help information.
Altitude. Show altitude (normal barograph trace) on vertical axis. Hopefully this is pretty self-explanatory. The vertical scale has a grid with gray lines at 1000 feet and darker lines at 5000 feet, or if meters are selected as units then 500m and 1000m respectively. Note that the horizontal axis can be changed to show distance around the task rather than time by using the button mentioned below.
Climb rate. Show climb rate on vertical axis.
Cruise speed. Show cruise speed on vertical axis.
Climb histogram. Show percentage of time spent climbing at each rate.

This is one of the most important screens in IGCview to really analyze your climbing performance. Your primary flight is represented by the blue line, while the other (secondary) flights for comparison are averaged together and represented by the shaded green area. The x-axis (horizontal) is divided into 'buckets' for each climb rate (if knots are the selected unit of climb rate then each vertical bar represents climbs of 0..1 knot, 1..2 knots, 3..4 knots etc. If you look carefully at the chart you'll see we have samples actually at half-knot intervals within each bucket, just to give slightly better detail. The strong vertical blue line shows your average climb rate for the task, e.g. in this example about 2.6 knots. The vertical green line is the average climb rate for the secondary log(s). Each gridline on the y-axis (i.e. the horizontal gridlines) represents 10%. This chart is a climb histogram, in that it shows you the percentage of time spent climbing at each of the defined rates. In this chart you can see I spent the largest amount of time (30%) climbing at 2..2.5 knots, while the competitor for comparison spent about 32% of the time climbing between 3.5 and 4 knots.
Expand Altitude. Stretch out altitude or climb rate on vertical axis.
Compress Altitude. Squeeze up altitude or climb rate on vertical axis.

Additional buttons which are displayed below the horizonatal axis affect that axis and are:

Time. Show time on horizontal axis (rather than distance, see below).
Distance. Show distance around task on horizontal axis. A task must be defined for this button to work.
Expand. Stretch out time or distance on horizontal axis.
Compress. Squeeze up time or distance on horizontal axis.
